# BAPTISTE — AI in the Present

**Duration:** ~4–5 min | **Section:** PART II — PRESENT
**Bridge:** From Lucas (HAL 9000, 1968) → to Thaïs (2025+)

---

## Core Argument

The AI of today didn't appear out of nowhere. It grew out of a chain of breakthroughs — image recognition, deep learning, transformers — that started in the 2000s and culminated in ChatGPT: the moment AI went from a lab experiment to something everyone uses every day.

---

## Key Points

### From the Past to the Present — The Bridge (2000s)
- After the AI winters and the symbolic milestones (Deep Blue, 1997), AI research accelerated with a new ingredient: **data**
- The internet was generating enormous amounts of text, images, and behavior data
- The question shifted from *can machines think?* to *can machines learn?*

### What is Machine Learning?
- Instead of programming rules manually → **the machine finds patterns on its own by training on examples**
- You don't tell it "a cat has pointy ears and whiskers" — you show it 10,000 photos of cats and it figures it out
- Hierarchy: **AI → Machine Learning → Deep Learning → LLMs**

### The Image Recognition Breakthrough — AlexNet (2012)
- **The key turning point:** a competition called ImageNet — 1.2 million labeled images, 1,000 categories
- In 2012, a team from the University of Toronto (**Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Geoffrey Hinton**) submitted **AlexNet**
- Result: error rate of **15.3%**, beating the runner-up by **10.8 percentage points** — no one had seen a gap like that
- It proved that deep neural networks trained on GPUs could outperform all hand-crafted methods
- **This is the moment the modern AI era began** — machines could now learn to see, almost at human level

### Deep Learning and Games
- **2016:** Google DeepMind's **AlphaGo** defeats Lee Sedol, the world Go champion, **4–1**
  - Go has more possible positions than atoms in the universe (10^170) — experts said AI couldn't master it for 10+ years
  - AlphaGo won a decade ahead of schedule
  - Move 37: a move with 1-in-10,000 probability — later called a stroke of genius

### The Transformer — The Architecture That Changed Everything (2017)
- In 2017, 8 Google researchers published **"Attention Is All You Need"**
- Innovation: **self-attention** — the model looks at all words simultaneously and understands their relationships
- Previous models processed words one by one → transformers process everything in parallel → much faster, much better at context
- The "T" in GPT stands for Transformer
- **Every major AI model since — GPT, Claude, Gemini — is built on this architecture**

### The Rise of LLMs
| Model | Date | Size | Key milestone |
|-------|------|------|---------------|
| GPT-1 | 2018 | 117M parameters | First transformer-based language model |
| GPT-2 | 2019 | 1.5B parameters | So convincing OpenAI refused to release it fully |
| GPT-3 | 2020 | 175B parameters | Generates human-like text, few-shot learning |
| GPT-4 | March 2023 | Multimodal | Understands text and images |

### The ChatGPT Moment — November 30, 2022
- OpenAI launches ChatGPT as a free research preview
- **1 million users in 5 days**
- **100 million users in 2 months** — the fastest-growing consumer application in history
  - TikTok took 9 months, Instagram took 2.5 years
- For the first time, ordinary people could talk to AI: students, writers, coders, doctors
- Today: **400 million weekly users** (early 2025)

### AI in 2024 — It's Everywhere
By 2024, **72% of companies** use AI in at least one business area. You interact with it every day:

| App / Tool | How AI is used |
|------------|---------------|
| Spotify | Recommends music based on your habits ("Discover Weekly") |
| Netflix | Recommends films, adapts thumbnails to you |
| Gmail | Smart compose, spam detection |
| Google Search | Understands meaning, not just keywords (BERT) |
| Face ID | Deep learning facial recognition |
| ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini | Write, code, explain, translate |
| Tesla / Waymo | Self-driving cars via computer vision |

### The Current Debates — Where Science Meets Ethics
- **Is AI conscious?** Current LLMs produce human-like responses — but is it understanding or pattern matching?
  - The "Chinese Room" (Searle): following rules perfectly without understanding meaning is not true intelligence
- **Geoffrey Hinton** — the "Godfather of AI", winner of the **2024 Nobel Prize in Physics** — quit Google in May 2023 to warn freely:
  - Risks: misinformation, autonomous weapons, unemployment, existential risk
  - *"I think it's quite conceivable that humanity is just a passing phase in the evolution of intelligence."*
- **EU AI Act** (August 2024) — the world's first comprehensive AI law
  - Bans social scoring, mass surveillance AI, subliminal manipulation
  - USA and UK did not sign the 2025 Paris declaration → global divide on regulation

>

>
> But the biggest technical shift came in 2017. Eight Google researchers published a paper called 'Attention Is All You Need.' They introduced the Transformer architecture — a new way for AI to process language by looking at all words simultaneously and understanding their relationships. This is the foundation of every language model you know today. The T in GPT stands for Transformer.
>
> And from there, language models grew exponentially. GPT-1 in 2018. GPT-2 in 2019 — so convincing that OpenAI refused to fully release it. GPT-3 in 2020, with 175 billion parameters, writing text indistinguishable from a human.

>
> So this is where we are in the present. AI has gone from a lab curiosity to something hundreds of millions of people use every day. The question is what comes next — and for that, I'll hand over to Thaïs."
