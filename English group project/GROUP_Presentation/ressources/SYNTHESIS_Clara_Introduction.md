# CLARA — Introduction: What is Intelligence?

**Duration:** ~3–4 min | **Section:** INTRO + KAHOOT

---

## Core Argument

To understand AI, we must first understand intelligence itself. AI researchers are trying to bring artificial intelligence as close as possible to human intelligence — but how close have they gotten, and what remains out of reach?

---

## Key Points

### Defining Intelligence
- **Definition:** The ability to learn from experience, solve problems, and use knowledge to adapt to new experiences

### 5 Components of Human Intelligence
1. Learn
2. Understanding
3. Reasoning
4. Adaptability
5. Emotional intelligence

### Types of Intelligence
- Analytical intelligence
- Creative intelligence
- Social intelligence
- Practical intelligence

### AI vs Human Intelligence
- AI developers have succeeded in reproducing several components:
  - Learn ✓
  - Understanding ✓
  - Reasoning ✓
  - Adaptability ✓
  - Emotional intelligence ✓ (partial)
- To measure AI intelligence, they use tests:
  - **Turing Test** — can a computer talk like a human?
  - **Logic Test / ARC Benchmark** — find correct output from pattern

### Turing Test (detail from research notes)
- Proposed by Alan Turing in 1950 (*Computing Machinery and Intelligence*)
- A human judge evaluates a conversation without seeing the players
- A computer passes if its conversation **cannot be easily distinguished from a human's**
- First to pass: **ELIZA** (1965, Weizenbaum) — a psychotherapy simulator
- Annual competition: **Loebner Prize** (winner 1997: Catherine — maintained conversation on one topic: Bill Clinton)

### Weak AI vs Strong AI vs Superintelligence
| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Weak AI** (current) | Operates on rule-based systems with predefined parameters. Unpredictable because it doesn't truly understand our world. |
| **Strong AI** | Exhibits self-awareness, creativity, and flexible, adaptive learning. Must have its own objectives and curiosity. |
| **Superintelligence** | Beyond human intelligence — theoretical horizon |

> We are currently at the **weak AI** stage. The presentation will trace the evolution of AI through the lens of science fiction.
